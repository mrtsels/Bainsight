# Problem Definition

## Context

Instant Retail Co. is currently trapped in a high-cost, low-density spiral in its Tier-3 and Tier-4 city operations. Unit economics are deeply negative, resulting in losses of ¥10–25M per city. These losses are driven by high fulfillment costs relative to low average order values (AOV), aggressive subsidy rates, and insufficient order density in these lower-tier markets.

Simultaneously, the competitive landscape is intensifying. Competitors are rapidly expanding warehouse and platform models (both dark stores and flash warehouses), threatening to commoditize the 30-minute delivery promise before Instant Retail Co. can secure customer loyalty. The company’s patience with these losses is running out, and leadership needs a definitive answer on where to play and how to win profitably—or if a strategic retreat is the most logical step.

## Key Question

> <a id="kq"></a>How can Instant Retail Co. turn its currently unprofitable Tier-3 and Tier-4 operations into a sustainable, profit-contributing engine that supports the company’s 3–5 year national ambition—without simply spending our way to scale?

## Scope

- **In scope**: Analysis of unit economics (AOV, subsidy rate, fulfillment cost, category margin), fulfillment operating models (dark store vs. flash/marketplace), scenario planning, and city-level portfolio prioritization for the 10 current T3-T4 cities.
- **Out of scope**: Multi-billion-dollar subsidy war strategies; operational optimizations for Tier-1 and Tier-2 cities (unless for contrast).
- **Time horizon**: 3-year path to positive contribution margin (supporting 3–5 year national ambition).

## Success Criteria

1. <a id="sc-city-logic"></a>**Quantified city-prioritization logic**: A focused portfolio approach based on leading indicators (order density trajectory, high-value user share, category mix, competitive white space) producing clear "invest / optimize / exit" recommendations.
2. <a id="sc-ue-model"></a>**Actionable unit economics model**: A simplified P&L distilling drivers to show the path to net profit per order and city-level profitability in 3 years.
3. <a id="sc-operating-model"></a>**Operating model and scenario clarity**: Explicit choices on fulfillment models and use cases (quick replenishment, fresh, ultra-convenience, night/lazy) that provide a right to win in T3-T4 markets.
4. <a id="sc-strategic-role"></a>**Coherent national strategic role**: Establishing a distinct purpose for T3-T4 markets (e.g., density moats, testing asset-light/partner models, demographic gateways).

## Constraints

- **Presentation ceiling**: Maximum 15 slides; must be high-level and story-driven, prioritizing insight over sheer completeness.
- **Data envelope**: Grounded strictly in the provided internal performance data for 10 cities and available public/analyst materials to triangulate market shifts (e.g., 50% O2O penetration surge).
- **Financial reality**: The break-even path must be self-funding or require only modest incremental investment. No multi-billion-dollar subsidy wars.
- **Time pressure**: Boardroom-ready, defensible storyline required within days.

## Stakeholders

| Stakeholder                  | Role                | Interest                                                                                                                                        |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Chief Strategy Officer (CSO) | Decision Maker      | Needs to separate signal from noise; requires a razor-sharp, honest assessment of where to play and how to win profitably (or when to retreat). |
| Executive Leadership         | Influencer/Approver | Needs to align the T3-T4 strategy with the 3-5 year national ambition without sustaining heavy financial bleed.                                 |

## Initial Hypotheses

1. <a id="hyp-city-portfolio"></a>**City Portfolio Pivot**: Applying a uniform strategy across all T3-T4 cities is failing; a focused portfolio approach (invest/optimize/exit) based on structural indicators will concentrate resources where profitability is viable.
2. <a id="hyp-operating-model"></a>**Asset-Light Migration**: The path to break-even requires migrating from high-cost, asset-heavy fulfillment (e.g., dark stores) toward lighter, partner-driven models (e.g., flash warehouse or marketplace) tailored to localized use cases.
