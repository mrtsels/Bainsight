# Recommendations: Tier-3/4 O2O Strategy

## Core Recommendation

<a id="rec-core"></a>

> **Execute a self-funded strategic reset in Tier-3/4 markets: Exit three structural loss-makers to free up capital, pivot the remaining seven cities to an asset-light Flash Warehouse model, and surgically taper subsidies to clear a 3-year path to profitable revenue growth.**

**Rationale**: Applying a uniform Tier-1 model to lower-tier cities destroyed value. By focusing only on markets where we hold a right to win and shifting to a lower-cost operating model, we immediately flip the unit economics. This enables sustainable, profitable top-line growth that the Board seeks, funded entirely by cutting the operational dead weight.
**Expected Impact**: Reallocates ~¥170M over three years from failing operations directly into high-ROI user acquisition and partner density in the remaining 7 cities.
**Confidence**: High

## Supporting Recommendations

### 1. Exit Xuzhou, Luoyang, and Ganzhou

**What**: Formally shutter Instant Retail Co. operations in these three cities. Convert any remaining users to a pure e-commerce model if applicable.
**Why**: These cities lack the density, market share, and high-value user penetration required for O2O profitability. They are structural sinkholes burning ~¥60M annually.
**Impact**: Instantly halts ~¥60M in annual operating losses.
**Timeline**: Months 0–3.
**Owner**: Operations & Finance.

### 2. Mandate a Flash Warehouse Transition in Surviving Cities

**What**: Aggressively unwind Dark Store leases in the 7 "Invest/Optimize" cities. Replace them by partnering with local supermarket chains and convenience stores to act as Flash Warehouses (partner picks, platform delivers).
**Why**: Dark stores require ~¥11-12 fulfillment cost per order, dooming the margin math at T3/4 volumes. Flash Warehouses drop this to ~¥9/order.
**Impact**: Structurally lowers the per-order break-even volume, transforming the 7 surviving cities into viable profit engines.
**Timeline**: Months 3–12.
**Owner**: Operations & BD.

### 3. Execute a Gated 20% Subsidy Taper

**What**: Cut per-order subsidies by 20% across the 7 surviving cities, shielding the top quartile of "high-value" users from the cuts.
**Why**: Current subsidies fund opportunistic, disloyal volume. By targeting the cuts at price-sensitive cohorts, total volume drops by <1% (optimistic scenario), while margins recover ¥0.7–1.0 per order.
**Impact**: Rapidly bridges the gap to unit profitability while protecting core revenue.
**Timeline**: Months 3–6 (Pilot) -> Months 6–12 (Rollout).
**Owner**: Marketing/Growth.

## Slide-Ready Summary

### Core Recommendation

Restructure the T3/4 portfolio to self-fund an asset-light, profitable revenue engine.

### Supporting Moves

1. Exit Xuzhou, Luoyang, Ganzhou — frees ~¥60M/year in dead capital.
2. Pivot to Flash Warehouses — lowers fulfillment baseline from ¥11 to ¥9.
3. Taper subsidies by 20% surgically — recovers margin with <1% volume loss.

### Implementation at a Glance

**Phase 1 (0-3m):**
• Shut down operations in bottom 3 cities.
• Unwind Dark Store leases in exit markets.
• Pilot 10% subsidy taper in one market.

**Phase 2 (3-12m):**
• Transition all dark stores to partner Flash Warehouses.
• Roll out 20% targeted subsidy reductions across 7 cities.
• Reinvest exit savings to drive revenue in top markets.

**Phase 3 (1-3y):**
• Optimize partner picking SLAs to defend consumer experience.
• Expand partner categories to capture AOV upside.

### The Stakes

• Turn a cash-burning footprint into a profitable, self-funding growth platform.
• Fail to act, and Tier-3/4 remains a structural drag on national margins.
