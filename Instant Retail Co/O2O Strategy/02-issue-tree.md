# Step 2: Issue Tree

> **Key Question**: How can Instant Retail Co. turn its currently unprofitable Tier-3 and Tier-4 operations into a sustainable, profit-contributing engine that supports the company’s 3–5 year national ambition—without simply spending our way to scale?

## Core Hypotheses Mapping

- **[Hypothesis 1: City Portfolio Pivot](01-problem-definition.md#hyp-city-portfolio)** maps to **Branch 1 (Where to Play)**: The assumption is that we are bleeding cash because we are fighting in all 10 cities equally. Moving to an Invest/Optimize/Exit model immediately stanches the bleeding in non-viable markets.
- **[Hypothesis 2: Asset-Light Migration](01-problem-definition.md#hyp-operating-model)** maps to **Branch 2 (How to Win) & Branch 3.3 (Fulfillment Costs)**: The assumption is that full dark-store economics will never work in lower-tier cities. Transitioning to flash warehouses or marketplace partnerships fundamentally reshaps the cost structure, enabling profitability even at lower density.
- **Subsidy Inefficiency Hypothesis** maps to **Branch 3.2 (Subsidy Efficiency)**: A massive driver of the ¥10–25M city-level loss is blunt-force user acquisition. Precision targeting of high-value user retention will drastically improve unit economics.
